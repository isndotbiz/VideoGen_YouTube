## üé∏ INSTRUMENTS EXPLAINED {#instruments}

### Best Instruments for Voiceover (200-800Hz Avoidance)

Human voice occupies **200-800 Hz** frequency range. Choose instruments that minimize this range:

#### ‚úÖ EXCELLENT (Minimal Conflict):

**1. Synths (High-Pass Filtered)**
- **Frequency Range:** 1000-8000 Hz (above voice)
- **Why Good:** Modern synths can sit above voice range
- **Best Types:** Pads, leads, arpeggios (bright)
- **Avoid:** Bass synths, mid-range leads
- **Examples:** Chillstep pads, minimal techno leads

**2. Bass (Sub-Bass Only)**
- **Frequency Range:** 20-100 Hz (below voice)
- **Why Good:** Adds weight without competing
- **Best Types:** Sub-bass, 808s, sine wave bass
- **Avoid:** Mid-bass (100-300 Hz)
- **Examples:** Electronic bass, synth bass (low-passed)

**3. Ambient Pads**
- **Frequency Range:** Variable, but can be filtered
- **Why Good:** Atmospheric, fills space, can avoid mids
- **Best Types:** String pads, synth pads, drones
- **Examples:** Ambient textures, atmospheric beds

**4. High Percussion**
- **Frequency Range:** 2000-12000 Hz (above voice)
- **Why Good:** Adds rhythm without masking voice
- **Best Types:** Hi-hats, shakers, cymbals (light)
- **Avoid:** Snares (200-500 Hz), toms

---

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è USE WITH CAUTION (Moderate Conflict):

**5. Piano (Played High)**
- **Frequency Range:** 200-4000 Hz (overlaps with voice)
- **Why Caution:** Can compete in mid-range
- **Best Use:** High register (above middle C), sparse playing
- **Avoid:** Dense chords in mid-range
- **Mitigation:** Use ducking mix, keep volume low (10-15%)

**6. Acoustic Guitar (Fingerpicking)**
- **Frequency Range:** 80-1000 Hz (some overlap)
- **Why Caution:** Body resonance in 200-400 Hz
- **Best Use:** High-register fingerpicking, light strumming
- **Avoid:** Heavy strumming, bass notes
- **Mitigation:** EQ cut at 200-400 Hz

---

#### ‚ùå AVOID (High Conflict):

**7. Electric Guitar (Rhythm)**
- **Frequency Range:** 200-2000 Hz (major overlap)
- **Problem:** Power chords dominate 200-800 Hz
- **When OK:** Lead guitar (high register only)

**8. Brass/Horns**
- **Frequency Range:** 150-1500 Hz (direct conflict)
- **Problem:** Trumpet, sax, brass dominate vocal range
- **When OK:** Very subtle, background horn sections

**9. Male Vocals/Choir**
- **Frequency Range:** 85-350 Hz (fundamental overlap)
- **Problem:** Direct competition with narration
- **Solution:** Never use vocals with voiceover

**10. Snare Drums/Claps**
- **Frequency Range:** 200-500 Hz (mid-range snap)
- **Problem:** Each hit competes with voice
- **When OK:** Very sparse, light, or filtered

---

### Instrument Combinations That Work

#### Perfect Combo 1: Minimal Electronic
```
Sub-Bass (20-80 Hz)
+ High Synth Pads (1000-4000 Hz)
+ Light Hi-Hats (8000-12000 Hz)
= Clear mid-range for voice
```

#### Perfect Combo 2: Corporate Minimal
```
Minimal Piano (High register: 1000-2000 Hz)
+ Soft Pads (500-3000 Hz, gentle)
+ Subtle Bass (40-100 Hz)
= Professional, unobtrusive
```

#### Perfect Combo 3: Ambient Meditation
```
Sustained Drones (100-500 Hz, very soft)
+ High Atmospheric Textures (2000-8000 Hz)
+ Nature Sounds (broadband, very quiet)
= Nearly invisible background
```

## üéπ INSTRUMENTS BY FREQUENCY RANGE {#instruments}

### Low Frequency (20-200 Hz) - SAFE FOR VOICEOVER ‚úÖ

**Instruments:**
- Sub-bass synths
- Kick drums (sub component)
- Bass guitar (low notes)
- Upright bass
- 808 bass
- Synth bass (low-pass filtered)

**Why Safe:** Below voice range, adds warmth without conflict

**Best Uses:** Electronic music, hip hop, lo-fi, minimal techno

---

### Mid-Low Frequency (200-500 Hz) - DANGER ZONE ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Instruments:**
- Male voice fundamentals (85-180 Hz + harmonics)
- Female voice fundamentals (165-255 Hz + harmonics)
- Acoustic guitar (body resonance)
- Piano (middle C area)
- Snare drums (body)
- Tom drums
- Bass guitar (upper notes)
- Cello (mid-range)

**Why Dangerous:** Direct overlap with voice clarity zone

**Mitigation:**
- Keep volume very low (10-15%)
- Use ducking mix
- Choose sparse arrangements
- EQ cut at 300-500 Hz

---

### Mid-High Frequency (500-2000 Hz) - MODERATE CAUTION ‚ö†Ô∏è

**Instruments:**
- Voice harmonics (intelligibility range)
- Piano (above middle C)
- Acoustic guitar (high frets)
- Electric guitar (leads)
- Violin/strings (mid-range)
- Synths (mid-range leads)

**Why Moderate:** Contains voice harmonics that affect clarity

**Mitigation:**
- Use minimal melodies
- Keep sparse, not dense
- High-pass filter >800 Hz when possible

---

### High Frequency (2000-20000 Hz) - SAFE FOR VOICEOVER ‚úÖ

**Instruments:**
- Hi-hats
- Cymbals (light)
- Shakers
- High synth leads
- Violin harmonics
- Piano (high octaves)
- Acoustic guitar (harmonics)
- Ambient textures (air, breath)

**Why Safe:** Above voice range, adds brightness and air without masking

**Best Uses:** All genres - adds interest without competing
